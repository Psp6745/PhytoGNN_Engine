🧠 GNN Computational Architecture

Our platform structures raw data into a heterogeneous biological network containing three classes of nodes (Plants, Phytochemicals, and Target Proteins). The connections represent relational pathways, mapping botanical origins to disease pathologies.

Graph Dimensions: 173 unique entity nodes connected by 424 relational edges.

Using Graph Convolutional Networks (GCN), the model aggregates structural characteristics from adjacent target pathways. Neurons pass messages across edges to calculate high-dimensional embeddings for botanical compounds.

Mathematical formula: H(l+1) = σ ( D^-1/2 * A * D^-1/2 * H(l) * W(l) )

Link Prediction computes the probability of a connection between a Chemical node and a Protein node. By using dot-product calculations on learned node embeddings, the AI suggests highly probable interactions that have not yet been discovered in laboratory settings.

Task: Predicting the therapeutic links of South Indian plants.

Explainable AI (XAI) explains the predictions made by the deep-learning model. By analyzing topological features and node relationships, the system provides clear explanations for why a specific chemical is predicted to target a given protein.

XAI output: Calculates local subgraph paths to verify predictions.

The Area Under the Receiver Operating Characteristic (AUC-ROC) score evaluates the model's performance. An AUC-ROC of 89.44% indicates that the AI has high accuracy and reliability in distinguishing true positive target links from random connections.

Accuracy metrics: Area Under Curve (AUC) = 89.44%
